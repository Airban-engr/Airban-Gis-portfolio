How Much Does Land Surveying Cost in Ghana? (2026 Guide)

If you are planning to buy land, build, or register property in Ghana, one of the most important steps is getting a professional land survey. But a common question many people ask is:

How much does land surveying cost in Ghana?

The cost of land surveying in Ghana varies depending on several factors, including location, land size, accessibility, and the type of survey required. In this guide, we break everything down so you know exactly what to expect.

---

Average Cost of Land Surveying in Ghana

On average, land surveying services in Ghana can range from:

  • GHS 2,000 – GHS 6,000+ for standard residential plots
  • Higher costs for large lands, complex terrains, or commercial projects

However, these prices are not fixed. The actual cost depends on the specifics of your land and the type of service required.

---

Factors That Affect Surveying Cost

1. Location

Surveying in urban areas like Accra, Kasoa, and Cape Coast may cost more due to demand and logistics compared to rural areas.

2. Land Size

The bigger the land, the more time and resources required, which increases the cost.

3. Type of Survey

  • Boundary / Cadastral Survey
  • Topographic Survey
  • Engineering Survey

Each type requires different levels of detail and equipment.

4. Terrain

Rocky areas, waterlogged zones, or heavily vegetated land will require more effort and may increase cost.

5. Documentation & Deliverables

Producing certified site plans, reports, and official documentation can also affect pricing.

---

Types of Surveying Services in Ghana

  • Cadastral Surveys – Defines land boundaries for ownership
  • Site Plans – Required for building permits
  • Topographic Surveys – Shows terrain and elevation
  • Engineering Surveys – Used for construction projects
---

Why You Should Not Choose the Cheapest Option

Choosing the cheapest surveyor can lead to serious problems such as:

  • Incorrect boundaries
  • Land disputes
  • Rejected building permits
  • Legal issues

A professional geomatic engineer ensures accuracy, legal compliance, and peace of mind.
