What Is a Topographic Survey?

A topographic survey maps the shape of the land â€“ including slopes, elevations, and key features such as buildings, walls, roads, drains, trees, and utilities. It provides a true picture of the site for:

  • Architectural and structural design
  • Road and drainage design
  • Platform and grading design
  • Flood and drainage assessment
  • Earthworks planning

What We Capture in a Topographic Survey

Depending on project needs, a topo survey may include:

  • Ground spot levels and contour lines
  • Existing buildings and structures
  • Roads, paths, and driveways
  • Drains, culverts, and watercourses
  • Boundary walls and fences
  • Significant trees and landscape features
  • Utility features where visible (poles, manholes, etc.)

Our Topographic Survey Process

  1. Project Brief: We discuss your design or planning needs to define what should be captured.
  2. Field Work: We visit the site with GNSS or total station equipment to record levels and features.
  3. Data Processing: We process field observations into a digital model with contours and spot levels.
  4. Plan & Digital Outputs: We prepare a clear topographic plan and can export data for CAD, GIS, or BIM workflows.

Who Benefits from Topographic Surveys?

  • Architects & Engineers: For designing buildings, platforms, and infrastructure.
  • Developers: For understanding site constraints before committing to projects.
  • Contractors: For planning earthworks and drainage.
  • Landowners: For understanding slopes and drainage risks on their property.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do you provide digital files for designers?

Yes. We can provide outputs in formats suitable for CAD, GIS, or other design tools, depending on your needs.

How long does a topographic survey take?

Fieldwork can often be completed in one or a few days, depending on the size and complexity of the site. Data processing and plan preparation follow shortly after.

Can a topographic survey help with drainage design?

Yes. Knowing the true levels and slopes is essential for drainage design, flood risk assessment, and siting of structures.
